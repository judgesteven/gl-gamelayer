Welcome to GameLayer's Gamification Guide!

We're excited to help you create a simple and fun gamification experience using GameLayer's dashboard. This guide will walk you through the basics, step by step. No need to worry about technical jargon—we've kept things simple. If you're after more technical details, you can always check out the API documentation for deeper insights.

Quick Overview of Gamification Mechanics

CHALLENGES

The heart and soul of the experience. Guide users towards action and how they will be rewarded.

STREAKS

Encourage users to return to your site daily by creating habitual use and embedding your service into daily routines.

EXPERIENCE POINTS

Use these to help users level-up within the experience and to compete against other users.

CREDITS

Can be earned for engaging with the experience and are used to claim the supported rewards.

LEVELS

Provide a sense of progression within the system and tailor the content experience accordingly.

LEADERBOARDS

Get your users competing against each other for the top rankings with periodic refreshes.

ACHIEVEMENTS

Recognition for when users do something awesome within the experience.

PRIZES

Motivation and incentive for users to continue engaging with the experience and spend their credits.

PRIZE DRAW

Periodic draws for users to enter with the possibility of winning much bigger prizes.

MYSTERY BOX

Instant wins with an element of risk... the mystery box may not award a prize this time.

QUIZZES

Build simple quiz experiences for fun or education and engage users longer with your content.

Our Demo: Building a Player-Friendly Onboarding Experience

Imagine you want to create an engaging onboarding for new users (who we'll call "players" from here on out). In this demo, we'll guide players through watching three product videos in a specific order to help them better understand your offerings.

Step 1: Tracking Player Actions with Events

The first thing we need is a way to track what our players are up to—specifically, when they watch one of the onboarding videos. For that, we need to set up an event.

This event will be the foundation for three different missions—one for each video. Each mission is completed when the event is triggered (and recorded by GameLayer through the API).

1. Navigate to the Events page

Find it in the left-hand menu

2. Click 'create' new Event

3. Expand the Event

Complete all the required fields

Step 2: Structuring Missions in the Right Order

We want to make sure our players watch the videos in the right order. So, we'll set up requirements that players need to finish Mission 1 (watching Video 1) before they can move on to Mission 2 (watching Video 2), and so on. It's a simple way to keep them on track!

1. Navigate to the Missions page

Find it in the left-hand menu

2. Click 'create' new Mission

3. Expand the Mission

Complete all the required fields

4. Set a unique identifier

This is used purely within GameLayer to reference the Mission

5. Give the Mission a Name

This is shown to players in the front-end

6. Add description and image (optional)

These are displayed to players in the front-end

7. Set active dates

Define when the Mission is active and if it should refresh periodically

8. Set the objective

Use the Event created earlier as the objective driving this Mission

9-10. Set rewards

Award the user points, credits and achievement steps upon completion

REQUIREMENTS - Used across different mechanics to structure content and personalize the player experience... missions, levels, achievements, category and tags can all be used as requirements

OBJECTIVES - All missions require one or many objectives to be completed... These objectives take the form of events and can be single or multiple. Internal quizzes and surveys can also drive missions.

Step 3: Adding Achievements for Motivation

Time to spice things up! Let's introduce an achievement called "Video Master." This achievement requires players to complete all three missions. For every mission they finish, they'll unlock one step toward this achievement.

After completing the first mission, the player will unlock the achievement, and with each following mission, they'll make more progress. You can even display this in your client app to encourage players with positive feedback. Everyone loves a good badge, right?

1. Navigate to the Achievements page

Find it in the left-hand menu

2. Click 'create' new Achievement

3. Expand the Achievement

Complete all the required fields

4. Set identification details

Give your Achievement a unique id, name, description and image

5. Define achievement steps

Set how many steps are needed to fully grant the achievement badge

6. Link to missions

Add the Achievement and the number of steps granted to each Mission that contributes towards it

Step 4: Connecting Missions to Achievements

It's super easy to connect mission completions to achievements. Just head over to the 'rewards' section in the dashboard and use the 'achievement-picker' to link missions to the achievements. You can even adjust how many steps each mission contributes toward an achievement—perfect for giving harder missions more weight!

POSITIVE FEEDBACK LOOP - We want players to know when they have done something positive within the systems and we use this to motivate and direct their continued engagement with the service.

Step 5: Levels and Levelling Up

Let's take things a step further by adding levels. Players earn experience points by completing missions, achievements, and other actions. In our demo, each mission completion gives players 10 points.

1. Navigate to the Levels page

Find it in the left-hand menu

2. Click 'create' new Level

3. Expand the Level

Complete all the required fields

4. Set identification details

Give your Level a unique id, name, description and image

5. Set starting level

Note, the ordinal for Levels starts at 0 and all players will start here as soon as they are active

6. Set level progression

Note Level Two has ordinal of 1 and requires 50 points (objective) for the player to reach

EXPERIENCE POINTS - As in video games, the more players engage with a service, the more expertise they gain... We measure that in points [XP] and use also, e.g. in player rankings and leaderboards

Bonus: Rewarding Players with Credits

When creating our "Video Master" achievement, we also set it to reward players with 10 credits once it's unlocked. Players can use these credits to claim rewards, like a prize from our simple prize management system.

For our demo, we've set up a prize that costs exactly 10 credits and can only be redeemed once per player. Easy, right?

1. Navigate to the Prizes page

Find it in the left-hand menu

2. Click 'create' new Prize

3. Expand the Prize

Complete all the required fields

4. Set identification details

Give your Prize a unique id, name, description and image

5. Set prize requirements

Define how many credits to redeem a prize, how many prizes a player can redeem, and available amount

6. Set availability period

Prizes require a period during which they are available and a refresh period for re-stocking

Advanced Game Design

We've created two levels: Level Uno and Level Dos. Every player starts at Level Uno and can level up to Level Dos after earning 50 points. Here's the twist: we've set our prize to only be available for players who have reached Level Dos or higher. Since completing all three missions only gives players 30 points, they'll need to find other ways to level up!

This is your chance to continue building the gamification experience beyond onboarding, engaging players in different ways.

And that's a wrap for our demo!
